SUMMARY OF ISSUES:
- 8 outright failures, primarily due to topic mismatch where the translation addresses
  a completely different subject than the Greek source.
- Several translations appear to be generic theological essays loosely associated with
  Isidore's themes but not actually translating the specific Greek text of the letter.
- Corrupted/garbled OCR in many source texts (especially letters >1100) makes
  verification difficult, but also means translations for those letters may have been
  fabricated rather than translated.
- Name mismatches are a strong signal of fabrication: when the Greek heading names
  one recipient but the translation names a different person (or no person), the
  translation was likely generated without reference to the actual source text.
- The 15 passing letters show high-quality, faithful translations that closely track
  the Greek vocabulary, structure, and argument. The quality gap between PASS and FAIL
  letters is stark, suggesting two different translation processes or batches.
