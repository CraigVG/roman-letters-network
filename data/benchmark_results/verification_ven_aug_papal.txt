1. FAIL — Augustine Ep. 218 (ID 148, to Palatinus):
   The English translation appears FABRICATED. The Latin exhorts Palatinus to
   persevere in Christian wisdom and not rely on his own strength. The English

   the source text.

========================================
RECURRING WARNINGS
========================================

1. COMPRESSION: Multiple collections show English translations at 10-30% of
   Latin length. This is most severe in Gelasius I (5-12% ratios) and
   Paulinus of Nola (13-27%). Some of this may be explained by critical
   apparatus in the Latin (especially Pelagius I and Gelasius I), but
   even accounting for that, translations appear heavily abridged rather
   than complete.

2. PELAGIUS I CRITICAL APPARATUS: The Latin text for Pelagius I letters
   includes heavy scholarly apparatus (manuscript collation notes, editorial
   references) mixed with the actual letter text. This makes verification
   harder and inflates Latin character counts.

3. GELASIUS I DUPLICATE CONTENT: Letters 4 (ID 6012) and 31 (ID 6169)
   share nearly identical openings ("Valde mirati sumus..."). Both deal
   with the Eutychian question. This may be legitimate (same encyclical to
   different recipients) but should be verified.

4. AUGUSTINE Ep. 124 (ID 101): Translation appears loosely paraphrased
   rather than closely translated, with generic content substituted for
   specific details in the Latin.

========================================
RECOMMENDATIONS
========================================

1. URGENT: Re-translate Augustine Ep. 218 (ID 148). Current translation
   is fabricated content unrelated to the source.

2. Review all Augustine translations for similar fabrication issues —
   the Ep. 124 loose paraphrase suggests this may not be isolated.

3. Consider expanding Gelasius I and Paulinus translations to reduce
   compression ratios.

4. Pelagius I Latin texts should ideally have critical apparatus stripped
   or the translations should be verified against apparatus-free editions.
